Mayo Clinic

Mayo Clinic is a renowned medical research institution, known for its commitment to innovative healthcare advancements. As a collaborative initiative between Arizona State University (ASU) and Mayo Clinic, it serves as a hub for cutting-edge research in various fields, including Computer Vision. The institution's dedication to pioneering medical technologies has attracted talented researchers and students, driving groundbreaking projects that have a significant impact on the healthcare industry.

During my tenure as a research intern at Mayo Clinic, I had the privilege of working under the guidance of Professor Imon Banerjee on a fascinating Computer Vision project. The project focused on wound sensing and treatment research, where we collaborated with NCSU to optimize sensors. By leveraging Python and OpenCV, I actively contributed to enhancing sensor image quality and accuracy through image registration, computer graphics techniques, and image processing. Our efforts resulted in a remarkable 75% improvement in image quality and accuracy, further advancing the field of Computer Vision and its potential applications in healthcare.

During my internship, I gained valuable skills that significantly improved my professional abilities as follows.

  • Utilized Python and OpenCV to implement image registration, computer graphics techniques, and image processing algorithms, resulting in an impressive 75% improvement in image quality and accuracy.
  • Documented the project's findings, methodologies, results, and recommendations, contributing valuable insights for future research and development in Computer Vision.
  • Applied theoretical knowledge to practical scenarios, honing critical problem-solving skills and gaining proficiency in technical implementation.
  • Collaborated effectively with a team of researchers at Mayo Clinic ASU, contributing to cutting-edge research and advancing healthcare technology.
  • Developed a deeper understanding of Computer Vision principles and applications, fueling my passion for this dynamic field and inspiring future pursuits in the domain.
  • Applied techniques like image overlay and heatmap generation to visualize differences in sensor images under various lighting conditions and from different devices, enhancing data analysis and interpretation.
